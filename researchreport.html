RMC Inc. Optimization Report for Chemical Blending

By Wilson Collins

Abstract

RMC Inc. seeks to determine the optimal blending process for two of their chemical products: a fuel additive and a solvent base. The goal is to maximize total profit while considering the constraints posed by the availability of raw materials. Using a linear programming model, this report identifies the ideal blending process to optimize production while maximizing profit.

Objective

RMC Inc. aims to determine the optimal production quantities for fuel additive and solvent base to maximize profit while adhering to material constraints.

Data Discussion

Fuel additive yields $40 per ton, while the solvent base provides $30 per ton. Each product requires different raw materials with specific constraints. The objective is to allocate resources efficiently to maximize profit.

Methodology

A linear programming model was employed to determine optimal production levels while considering material constraints. The objective function is represented as:

Maximize Profit = 40X + 30Y

Subject to:

  • 2/5X + 1/2Y ≤ 20 (Material 1)
  • 1/5Y ≤ 5 (Material 2)
  • 3/5X + 3/10Y ≤ 21 (Material 3)

Analysis Results

The optimal production levels were determined to be 25 tons of fuel additive and 20 tons of solvent base, yielding a total profit of $1,600. Material 1 and 3 were fully utilized, while Material 2 was used at 80% capacity.

Suggestions

  • Increase product prices to $60 per ton for fuel additive and $50 per ton for solvent base to boost profit.
  • Invest in more raw materials, particularly Material 1 and 3, to expand production capabilities.

Summary

RMC Inc. should adjust pricing strategies and expand resource acquisition to enhance profitability. This approach will ensure competitiveness and maximize resource utilization.
